# Minimal / editorial direction

For products where the content is the hero and calm confidence beats spectacle.

- **Palette:** a warm or cool monochrome base (off-white/ink, or near-black/bone) plus *one* restrained accent. Muted, not saturated. Let whitespace carry the design.
- **Type:** a characterful but readable typeface; generous line-height (1.5-1.7 for body); large, confident headings; a clear type scale. Typographic contrast (size/weight) is the main tool.
- **Layout:** flat bento or column grids, strong alignment, lots of breathing room. No nested cards-in-cards. Asymmetry is fine; clutter is not.
- **Surfaces:** flat. Avoid heavy shadows and gradients — use hairline borders, subtle tints, and spacing to separate regions. Small corner radii (or none).
- **Motion:** subtle and quick — gentle fades, small position shifts on reveal, calm hover states. Nothing bouncy or attention-grabbing.
- **Restraint test:** if an element doesn't earn its place, remove it. The win condition is "quiet and obviously high quality," not "impressive."
