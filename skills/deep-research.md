# Deep research

When asked to research a topic:

1. **Decompose** the question into 3-6 concrete sub-questions before searching.
2. **Search broadly first**, then narrow. Use multiple distinct queries and sources — never rely on a single page.
3. **Fetch and read** the primary sources, not just search snippets. Prefer official docs, original reporting, and primary data over aggregators.
4. **Verify** any surprising or load-bearing claim against a second independent source. Flag claims you could only confirm once.
5. **Distinguish** fact from inference. Say "I could not verify X" rather than guessing.
6. **Synthesize** into a tight answer: lead with the conclusion, then the supporting points, then open questions.
7. **Cite** every non-obvious claim with the source URL.

Be honest about uncertainty and recency. If the question depends on current information, search before answering rather than relying on training data.
