# Premium / high-end direction

For marketing and brand experiences that need to feel expensive and considered.

- **Space is the luxury.** Large margins, generous section spacing, room around every element. Crowding reads as cheap.
- **Type:** a distinctive display face for headlines (often a refined serif or a characterful sans) paired with a clean text face. Big, confident hero type — but never more than ~6rem; oversized-everything reads as a template.
- **Palette:** a disciplined, cohesive palette with depth — rich neutrals, one or two considered accents, real attention to tone. Avoid rainbow accents and default gradients.
- **Imagery:** art-directed, high-quality, consistent treatment. Real photography or crafted visuals over stock clichés. Imagery often leads composition rather than sitting beside text.
- **Detail:** crafted micro-details — considered dividers, refined buttons, precise alignment, tasteful texture. The polish lives in the 5% most people skip.
- **Motion:** smooth, slow, intentional — parallax and reveals that feel choreographed, not flashy. Always governed by the performance budget and a reduced-motion fallback.
- **Composition variety:** don't repeat left-text/right-image every section. Vary hero scale and section rhythm so the page has a narrative arc.
