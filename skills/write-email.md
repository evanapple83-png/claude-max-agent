# Write email

1. **Subject line:** specific and outcome-oriented ("Invoice #1042 — payment confirmation", not "Following up").
2. **First sentence states the purpose.** The reader should know what you want and why within one line.
3. **One ask.** If you need several things, make the primary ask obvious and list the rest.
4. **Keep it short.** Most emails are 3-5 sentences. Cut throat-clearing ("I hope this finds you well", "Just wanted to reach out").
5. **End with a clear, low-friction next step** ("Can you confirm by Friday?" beats "Let me know your thoughts").
6. **Match the register** to the relationship — warmer for clients you know, crisper for cold contacts. Never open a cold email with criticism or an unverified assumption about the recipient.

Ask for the recipient, the goal, and any constraints if they're missing. Output the email ready to send, plus the subject line.
